Recipe

Hasenpfeffer-Rabbit-Stew

Ingredients

  • One large rabbit, dressed, and cut into pieces
  • ½ cup flour
  • ½ teaspoon garlic powder
  • ½ teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon orange rind
  • ¼ cup olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon pepper
  • ½ teaspoon allspice or cloves
  • 1 medium-sized onion
  • 5 cloves garlic, peeled and sliced
  • 2 bay leaves
  • ½ cup vinegar
  • 1 can tomato puree

Instructions

Combine garlic powder, onion powder, and flour. Roll pieces of meat in flour mixture, and fry in the oil until golden brown. Place the fried meat in a Dutch oven with the sliced onion, garlic, allspice, bay leaves, salt, pepper, vinegar, and tomato puree. Bake at 350 for 1 to 1.5 hours.

My friend and I ate at this establishment the other day and I was very impressed. I ordered the hasenpfeffer which is a rabbit stew braised in a tangy sauce and my friend ordered the cheese dumplings with mushroom broth. I have no complaints about the food because it was exceptionally delicious. Our only complaint is that this restaurant is located next to a farm, and we happened to be dining on the outdoor patio. We were surrounded by a flock of about 10-12 chickens that would not stop pestering us for food. Overall, the food was great, and I would eat here again, but I recommend dining indoors. I took one star off for the unruly chickens.
